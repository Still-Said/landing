The problem

4,000 messages still feels like a wall

Years of messages — ordinary check-ins, inside jokes, late-night calls reduced to text, the specific way they said your name — become one flat archive. Too meaningful to delete. Too overwhelming to actually go back into.

The format does not know the difference between a forwarded meme and a moment that mattered. It cannot surface the phrases they used a hundred times, the jokes that were specifically yours, or the way they showed up in small messages across years. You have to hold all of that yourself.

Most people do not go back. They keep the archive because deleting feels wrong, and they leave it there.

What StillSaid does

We turn your messages into something worth keeping

Step one

You share context

Tell us about the conversation — who's in it, how they spoke, what languages they mixed, what you most want preserved, and what you would like us to avoid.

Step two

We curate the archive

We run your messages through purpose-built curation lenses — recurring patterns, warmth and humor, hidden gems, and the shifts that marked the relationship. Every surfaced moment links back to original messages.

Step three

You review what gets kept

Nothing goes into the final archive without your sign-off. You control what's included, how it's titled, and how the language is handled. Every moment is source-backed. Nothing is invented.

Who this is for

It started with the hardest case

The first keepsake was built from messages with someone who had died — years of conversations that were too painful to read through and too important to leave buried. That is still the sharpest version of the problem.

But it is not the only one. A family group where three generations talk over each other. A friend group from college that has fifteen years of inside jokes. A team chat from a job that mattered. A retirement gift. A wedding gift. A parent who is still here and has no idea how much of themselves they have put into messages over the years.

If the archive matters, the keepsake is worth making. You do not have to be grieving to want it.

What makes it different

Not a summary. Not a chatbot. Not a transcript.

Not this

A generic AI summary

We do not flatten years of conversation into vague takeaways about how much someone "cared deeply." We do not explain the relationship, interpret what someone meant, or draw conclusions about how they felt. Every moment in the archive is grounded in what was actually said — curated, not narrated.

Not this

A simulation or chatbot

We do not generate new messages, recreate the person, or put words in anyone's mouth. The archive is a record of what happened, not a continuation of it.

Not this

A printed export

Raw preservation keeps everything but helps you find nothing. We surface what mattered across thousands of messages — the patterns, phrases, and moments that would otherwise stay invisible.

Not this

Something out of your hands

You tell us what to look for and what to avoid. You review every moment before it enters the archive. You decide what gets kept, renamed, or left out.

What the archive can recover

What you can ask us to surface

You tell us what matters most. The curation adapts — four structural lenses run on every archive, plus a personal lens built from your answers.

Recurring patterns

Phrases, sign-offs, pet names, verbal habits repeated across hundreds of messages. The things that were specifically theirs.

Warmth & humor

Running jokes, typos, invented words, exchanges that made you laugh — the moments that were light and alive.

Hidden gems

One-off striking moments — a compliment, an admission, a memory shared once. The things you forgot were in there.

Shifts

Life transitions visible in the archive — changes in tone, frequency, or content marking a before and after.

Privacy & trust

Built for sensitive personal archives

You are uploading something irreplaceable. Here is exactly how we handle it.

Never used to train any model

Your archive is not used to improve any AI system — ours or anyone else's. Non-negotiable. Ever.

Never shared or sold

Your archive and your intake responses are not shared with third parties under any circumstances.

Raw archive deleted after processing

Once your curated output is ready, the original export is deleted. Only what you choose to keep is stored.

You control what gets surfaced

You tell us what topics to avoid. Nothing goes into the final archive without your review and sign-off.

How early access works

A personal process, not a self-serve app

Right now, StillSaid is working closely with a small number of people to create the first keepsakes carefully. That lets us get the output right before automating more of the workflow.

1

Tell us about them

A short intake — who this person or group is, how they communicated, what languages they mixed, the people who came up in messages, what you're hoping to find.

2

Share your archive securely

Upload your message export. We'll walk you through how. Your archive is processed privately and deleted after your keepsake is ready.

3

Receive your keepsake

We curate 25–70 moments depending on your archive size — recurring phrases, warmth, hidden gems, and the shifts that marked the relationship. Every moment links back to the original messages.

4

Review and tell us what feels right

You read through. Anything that doesn't belong, you remove. The final keepsake reflects what you want kept — in their words, exactly as they wrote them.
